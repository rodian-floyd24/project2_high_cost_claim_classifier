# Explainability Methodology

Reason codes summarize model-input patterns that are actuarially plausible risk indicators, including chronic burden, prior inpatient use, utilization intensity, provider fragmentation, prior high-cost history, and partial-year enrollment.

Reason codes are descriptive, not causal. They explain why a profile appears elevated relative to the feature logic and should be reviewed alongside calibration, top-k performance, and data-quality flags.
